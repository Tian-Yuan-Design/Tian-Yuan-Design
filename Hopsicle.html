Service Design HOPSICLE

PROJECT

A service design work which enhance share-use concept in HK urban promenade, change people's mindset with bicycle.

TEAM

Claire GUAN
Holly, Yi HAO
Kelly, Yuan TIAN
Tania Jocelynn Trisnadi

ROLE

Designer

In this project, we were invited to design new service for BiciLine in the 6 months(2018.06 - 2018.12). We need to design new service which can reveal more values in cycling, promote "share-use" concept, and create a greeway with shared space in urban promenade.

Finally, we design a series of activities, a mobile APP, and a smart device installed on bike.

· Background ·

Hong Kong has strict policies that discourage people to use bicycle as transportation and/or leisure tool.
BiciLine has to create a concept to introduce bicycle to public as a tool of many values and  one that would fit and enhance the shared-use concept in public spaces.
Share-use concept means, all people in the urban promenade, includes walkers, joggers, cyclists, tourists, etc. can share the public space together without colliding.

· Research Methods ·

· Insights ·

Customize Experience

Kids see more possibilities in how the space and facilities ca be used. They enjoy the park by customizing their own experiences.

Collaborate Activity

When using a shared facility, people tend to have a "me against the world" mentality, lenient to oneself, strict toward others.

Escape the Mundane

People go to the park to escape their daily routines. They tend to be more open to explore new activities and unique experiences, more communal than individual.

Evoke Nostalgia

There's a nostalgic aspect to biking because biking is a childhood memory shared by many. Kwun Tong Promenade design and location has an underlying theme of old Hong Kong memories.

· Ideation ·

Based on the research results, previous user journey map, we brainstorm and come up with bunches of ideas. After synthesizing, our final concept is a basket of activity service, mobile app and smart devices installed with bikes.
