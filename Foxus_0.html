FOXUS is designed as MOOC (Massive Open Online Course). In this project, we want to think about what the MOOC's position should be, reframe the users and their needs, and design FOXUS as their solution. Also, FOXUS will be a cross-platform product, which can be used on laptop, mobile phone, smart TV and smart watch.

"FOXUS ≠ Online University; FOXUS ≠ Online Training Classes"

· User Archetypes ·

I did some research and analyzed target users — who prepare to “study” via FOXUS, and classify the scenarios and motivations into three types.

Knowledge-Oriented

Which means that users aim to learn or dive deeper in a specific subject professionally. For example: Sam is a sophomore in mathematic, and wants to deepening the knowledge of linear algebra.

Job-Oriented

Which means that users aim to get pragmatic skills to cope with their works, or find career opportunities. For example: Sam is a programer now, and wants to learn machine learning so that get a better salary.

Enjoyment-Oriented

Which means that users intend to keep an open mind and enjoy know something new without specific purposiveness. For example, Sam traveled Italy recently, and was super fascinated with this country. He wants to know more culture and history about Italy.

Why I do SCENARIOS like this?
People have various purposes and expectations which they come to MOOC and choose courses. I need to understand it and design the information architecture and “charming features” of the new MOOC. Especially after competitive research, I found:
Currently, most MOOC platforms only offer bunches of courses for users to search and get course list without customization, to choose one to enroll haphazardly, to learn by watching videos and filling out test paper mechanically. Also, the courses offered on MOOC are classified with subject, university and professor — as what happened in real colleges. It's suitable for "Knowledge-Oriented" users. But how about the other two kinds of users? Different targets makes users think different, seek different, learn in different way.

· Insights for FOXUS ·

To meet these three types of users’ needs, I consider FOXUS should have characters like:

explore and get

matchable courses

According to different user scenarios, I need to categories courses properly, to present suitable courses’ introduction, to recommend customized courses. I hope to create explorative atmosphere in FOXUS, rather than the atmosphere like shopping in mall and selecting things from shelves.

learn at

anytime anywhere

It’s the trend that people in Morden cities have sliced their time into many fragmented time, and shift between various places frequently. So FOXUS should consider both how to improve users’ learning experience and how to smooth their learning feeling in different places.

progress with

like-minded

No one should be an island. For knowledge-oriented users, they need “classmates” to discuss with. For job-oriented users, they need “fellows” to communicate with. So FOXUS provide the platform to users to meet with like-minded people and progress together.

· Information Architecture ·

Before thinking about the information architecture, we design user flow firstly. Sign up - Search courses - Choose courses - have classes - After classes. Based on user flow, we think about their action, purpose, mood to instruct our later design.

Then, we think about what should different terminals(Phone, Web, Smart Watch, Smart TV) do to realize our expectation to FOXUS, and define the information architecture.
